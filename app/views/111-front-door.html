These are all the issues covered by legal aid. Choose the problem you most need help with.

  • Children, families and relationships
    Includes social services, children in care; divorce or separation, disputes about children and finances; domestic abuse.
  • Domestic abuse, harassment
    Includes abuse, threats, harassment, stalking, made to feel unsafe by a partner, ex-partner or someone else; worried about a child.
  • Housing, homelessness, losing your home
    Includes being evicted, forced to sell your home; problems with landlords, council housing, repairs or neighbours.
  • Care for old age, health issues or disability; mental capacity
    Includes getting care or funding from the council or NHS; problems with quality of care; help if someone cannot look after themselves.
  • Schools, education, special educational needs and disability (SEND)
    Includes EHCP, needs assessments, SEND tribunals; unfair treatment at school due to SEND; judicial reviews.
  • Racism, sexism, homophobia, other discrimination
    Treated unfairly because of your race, sex, disability or health condition, religion, age, sexual orientation, gender, pregnancy or maternity.
  • Sexual assault, child abuse, forced marriage, FGM
    Worried about someone related to forced marriage or FGM; claims for sexual assault or child abuse.

Other problems

  • Appeal a benefits decision
    Only for appeals to the Upper Tribunal (Administrative Appeals Chamber).
  • Ayslum, immigration, trafficking, modern slavery
    Includes applying for asylum; staying in the UK if you’re a victim of domestic abuse; immigration detention bail.
  • Legal action against public bodies
    Police, government, prisons, NHS, local authorities, courts or other organisations that have power to make decisions about you.
  • Mental health detention
    If you’ve been detained under the Mental Health Act (sectioned); community treatment orders; mental health tribunals; Court of Protection.
